Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis

The most famous Lithuanian composer and artist of all time

Timeline of Mikalojus Konstantinas Čiurlionis Life

  • 1875 - Born in Senoji Varėna
  • 1880 - At the age of five plays piano by ear and can sight-read music freely by the age of seven
  • 1889-1893 - Studies at Plungė Palace Orchestra School
  • 1894-1899 - Studies piano and composition at Warsaw Institute of Music
  • 1899 - For his graduation, he writes a cantata for mixed chorus and symphonic orchestra titled De Profundis, and receives the diploma for composition specialty.
  • 1900 - Finishes his first symphonic poem In the Forest
  • 1901 - Joines the Leipzig Conservatory and studies composition, aesthetics, hostory and psychology. Also, he starts drawing during his free time.
  • 1902 - Returns to Warsaw, joines the Warsaw Drawing school, and works as a private music tutor.
  • 1903 - Paints the 7 painting cycle Funeral Symphony, but also doesn't forget music: he starts creating his second symphonic poem The Sea.
  • 1904-1906 - Continues his painting studies in Warsaw School of Fine Arts.
  • 1905 - Participates in the first annual exhibition of Warsaw School of Arts. Goes on a trip in Europe visiting Prague, Dresden, Nuremberg, Munich, Vienna. He adores the works of Van Dyck, Rembrandt, Böcklin.
  • 1906 - Participates in the exhibition of Warsaw's School of Fine Arts in Saint Petersburg. Articles start appearing in the press about M. K. Čiurlionis.
  • 1907 - Moves to Vilnius and actively involves himself in the cultural life. Helps organising and participates in the First Exhibition of Lithuanian Art at Vileišis Palace, Vilnius with his 33 paintings. Soon after this event, the Lithuanian Art Society was founded, and Čiurlionis was one of its 19 founding members. Same year he meets the writer Sofija Kymantaitė.
  • 1908 - Trying to become more known, he folowes his friends' advice and leaves for Saint Petersburg.
  • 1909 - Maries Sofija Kymantaitė.
  • 1909 - Maries Sofija Kymantaitė. After the wedding and parent visits, both newlyweds come to Saint Petersburg. Here M. K. Čiurlionis paintings are exhibited in Salon exhibition, in the Sixth Exhibition of Russian Artist Society, among them - his newest painting - Rex.
  • 1910 - After the burnout together with his wife Ciurlionis comes back to Lithuania, Druskininkai. Later he is hospitalized in a health resort in Poland. While he is getting better, his only daughter - Danutė - was born.
  • 1911 - Gets pneumonia and dies at the age of 35.

Čiurlionis felt that he was a synesthete; that is, he perceived colours and music simultaneously. Čiurlionis stands out in the world context because he used musical forms in painting. According to this, he is absolutely unique in the world.
